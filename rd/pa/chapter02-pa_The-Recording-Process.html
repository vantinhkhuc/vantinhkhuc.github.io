Session 2

Steps to Journalize a Transaction

  1. Date: Enter the date in the date column
  2. Debit: Write debit account title at left margin, amount in debit column
  3. Credit: Write credit account title indented, amount in credit column
  4. Explanation: Write brief explanation below credit account
  5. Space: Leave a blank line between entries

The Ref. column is left blank until posting!

What is Posting?

Definition: Transferring journal entries to ledger accounts

Why Post?

  • Accumulates effects of transactions by account
  • Shows current balance of each account
  • Organizes information for financial statements
Session 2

Posting Steps

  1. Post Debit: Transfer debit amount to debit side of ledger account
    • Include date and journal page
  2. Record Reference: Write account number in journal Ref. column
  3. Post Credit: Transfer credit amount to credit side of ledger account
    • Include date and journal page
  4. Record Reference: Write account number in journal Ref. column

What is a Trial Balance?

Definition: A list of accounts and their balances at a given time

Primary Purpose:

Prove that debits equal credits after posting

Additional Benefits:

  • Helps discover errors in journalizing and posting
  • Useful for preparing financial statements
  • Shows all account balances in one place

Steps to Prepare a Trial Balance

  1. List accounts in order (Assets, Liabilities, Equity, Revenues, Expenses)
  2. Enter balances in appropriate column:
    • Debit balances ‚Üí Debit column
    • Credit balances ‚Üí Credit column
  3. Total the debit and credit columns
  4. Verify that totals are equal

If totals don't match, you have an error!

Session 3

What Trial Balance PROVES

‚úì Trial Balance DOES Prove:

  • Debits equal credits
  • Mathematical equality after posting
  • Ledger is in balance
Session 3

What Trial Balance DOES NOT Prove

‚úó Trial Balance Does NOT Guarantee:

  • All transactions were recorded
  • Transactions were recorded in correct accounts
  • Transactions were recorded for correct amounts
  • The ledger is error-free

A balanced trial balance ‚â† No errors!

Session 3

Errors NOT Detected by Trial Balance

  1. Transaction not journalized

    Example: Forgot to record a sale

  2. Correct journal entry not posted

    Example: Journalized but never transferred to ledger

  3. Journal entry posted twice

    Example: Same entry posted on Monday and Tuesday

  4. Wrong accounts used

    Example: Debited Equipment instead of Supplies

  5. Offsetting errors

    Example: Two different $500 errors that cancel out

Session 3

Locating Errors in Trial Balance

Step-by-Step Error Detection:

  1. Calculate difference between debit and credit totals
  2. If difference is 1, 10, 100, or 1,000:
    • Re-add the columns
  3. If difference is divisible by 2:
    • Look for amount equal to half the difference in wrong column
  4. If difference is divisible by 9:
    • Look for transposition error (e.g., 54 written as 45)
  5. Check if any account balance was omitted
Session 3

Professional Formatting Rules

Currency Signs:

  • Show currency sign for first item in column
  • Show currency sign for totals
  • Do NOT use in journals or ledgers

Underlining:

  • Single line = subtotal or amount to be added/subtracted
  • Double line = final total

Application to E-commerce

How Recording Process Applies to Online Business:

  • Daily Sales: Record online transactions (Debit: Cash/Accounts Receivable, Credit: Sales Revenue)
  • Inventory Purchases: Track supplier payments (Debit: Inventory, Credit: Cash/Accounts Payable)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Reversing debits and credits

    Always check: Does this account increase or decrease?

  2. Using wrong account

    Equipment vs. Supplies, Cash vs. Accounts Receivable

  3. Omitting the credit entry

    Remember: Every debit needs a credit!

  4. Mathematical errors

    Double-check your calculations

  5. Transposition errors

    Writing 54 instead of 45
